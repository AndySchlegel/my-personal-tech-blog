My Story

For almost two decades, I worked in sales. Key Account Manager, finance, real estate, car sales. Before that, six years in the Bundeswehr. Technology was always something I used, but never something I truly understood on a deeper level.

In March 2025, I started a retraining program called “Java DevOps Engineer” through CloudHelden. 2,300 hours of training. Four modules. Zero prior IT knowledge — just a gut feeling that this was the right move.

The first weeks were abstract. Lots of theory, lots of “why does this matter?” But around September 2025, something clicked. I started building things beyond the curriculum. A NAS became a homelab. A homelab became a multi-server infrastructure across four environments. Problems became puzzles I actually wanted to solve.

Then in early 2026, my NAS MongoDB got cryptomined. My Hetzner server got compromised through an open Portainer port. These were not setbacks — they were the best learning experiences I could have asked for. Real incidents, real forensics, real hardening.

One year later: three certifications (with a fourth in progress), a production AWS project, 50+ Docker services across four environments, real incident response experience, and this blog. Still learning every day.

“I don’t just deploy infrastructure — I defend it. Getting hacked twice taught me more than any certification ever could.”
